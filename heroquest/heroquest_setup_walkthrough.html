HeroQuest Game Setup and First Quest Walkthrough

Think of HeroQuest setup like preparing for a live stage performance: you have a script, a cast, and a stage. Everything must be placed just right to deliver the right dramatic impact. Here's how to prepare your dungeon stage for the opening act.

Unboxing and Game Pieces

Imagine opening a toolbox before fixing a machine. Each piece in HeroQuest serves a purpose: doors, miniatures, furniture, cards, dice, and the game board. Like a mechanic with parts laid out before an engine rebuild, a good HeroQuest setup starts with sorting.

  • Game board - the dungeon layout with rooms and corridors
  • Miniatures - heroes, monsters, and Zargon's minions
  • Furniture - bookcases, chests, altars, tables (adds narrative and obstacles)
  • Cards - spells, equipment, treasure, and monsters
  • Dice - special combat dice and movement dice

Setting the Stage with the Game Board

The board is like a blank film set—Zargon populates it with actors and props depending on the quest. The heroes only see one room at a time, so the board remains mostly empty at the start. As they move through doors, Zargon reveals what's inside.

The dotted line above represents unexplored corridors. Players don’t know what lies ahead until they "open the door."

Starting a Quest: The Adventure Begins

The first quest, “The Trial,” is like a training mission in a video game. It introduces all core mechanics: moving, attacking, searching for treasure, and casting spells.

Let’s break it down like a movie scene:

sequenceDiagram participant Barbarian participant Dwarf participant Zargon Barbarian->>Zargon: I open the door Zargon->>Barbarian: Reveals orc and chest Barbarian->>Orc: Attacks with sword Dwarf->>Room: Searches for traps Zargon->>Room: Reveals pit trap

Every turn includes:

  • Moving (rolling 2d6)
  • Attacking an enemy (rolling combat dice)
  • Optional action: searching for traps, doors, or treasure

Hero Stats Breakdown

Each hero has their own "RPG-style character sheet." Think of it like choosing a Pokémon or RPG class—some are tanky, some are squishy spellcasters, some are fast and clever.

Quest Objective

In “The Trial,” the heroes must explore the dungeon, defeat monsters, avoid traps, and find a route to safety. There is no single boss—just a gauntlet of challenges. It’s like a pilot episode where the heroes learn to work together.

Along the way, they’ll face:

  • Orcs – basic fighters
  • Goblins – weak but fast
  • Fimir – tougher foes
  • Traps – pit traps, spear traps
  • Treasure – gold, potions, gear upgrades

Player Actions: A Turn-by-Turn Metaphor

Think of each hero’s turn like a chess move. You don’t just go forward—you plan for what might be revealed. Will you block a hallway and protect your wizard? Will you check for traps before opening that treasure chest?

Every decision is like a page in a storybook: irreversible and impactful. Teamwork makes the dungeon crawl successful, not just brute strength.

Ending the First Quest

Once the heroes complete the objective or escape, they record their loot, update character sheets, and prepare for the next quest. Like finishing a level in a game, you keep your progress and move to the next chapter.
